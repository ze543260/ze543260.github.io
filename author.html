Author

Jack London was an American novelist, short story writer, essayist, journalist, and social activist. He is best known for his novels **The Call of the Wild** and **White Fang**, both of which are set in the Yukon Territory during the Klondike Gold Rush. London was born in San Francisco in 1876. He was raised in poverty and had a difficult childhood. He left school at the age of 14 to work as a sailor, miner, and journalist. London's writing often explored themes of nature, social class, and the American dream. He was a prolific writer and published over 50 books during his lifetime. He died in 1916 at the age of 40.
